Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 10, 2026  ·  Version 1.0

1. Overview

2doc ("the app", "we", "us") is a document-creation tool that reads data already on your device — messages, call logs, contacts, calendar events, and device info — and helps you export it as a PDF. Your privacy is central to how the app is designed: all source data stays on your device and is never uploaded to our servers.

This policy explains exactly what we collect, what leaves your device, and who else may receive information about you.

2. Data that stays on your device

The following sources are read locally to build your documents. Nothing from these sources is transmitted to us or any third party:

  • SMS / MMS text messages and media on your device
  • Call log call history (number, duration, direction)
  • Contacts names, phone numbers, email addresses
  • Calendar events from calendars you select when building a document
  • Device info hardware model, OS version, storage, installed apps
  • Telegram messages and media accessed via the Telegram API on your behalf
  • WhatsApp exported chat files you provide to the app

Generated PDFs are saved to your device's local storage. They are never uploaded unless you choose to share them.

3. Data that leaves your device

The app uses the following services, each of which may receive limited information:

Firebase (Google)

We use Firebase Analytics to understand feature usage (e.g. "user exported a PDF") and Firebase Crashlytics to receive crash reports. Analytics events contain no personal content from your messages or files — only anonymised usage signals and a randomly generated device identifier.

RevenueCat

Manages purchase state. RevenueCat receives your app-store purchase receipt and a pseudonymous user ID to verify Pro access. It does not receive any content from your device sources.

Telegram

When you connect a Telegram account, the app communicates directly with Telegram's API using your credentials to retrieve messages. Your Telegram session is stored locally on your device only. We never see your Telegram password or messages.

4. Permissions

The app requests the following Android permissions only to provide its core functionality:

  • READ_SMS / READ_MMS — to read SMS and MMS messages
  • READ_CALL_LOG — to read the call history
  • READ_CONTACTS — to read contact names and details
  • READ_CALENDAR — to read calendar events
  • READ_EXTERNAL_STORAGE / MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE — to read WhatsApp exports you select and to write generated PDFs
  • INTERNET — to connect to Telegram's API and to send analytics events and crash reports
  • BILLING — to process the one-time Pro purchase via Google Play

5. Data retention

We do not store your device data on any server. Crash reports are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for 90 days. Analytics data is retained according to Google's standard Firebase Analytics retention settings (default: 14 months). RevenueCat retains purchase records as required for purchase verification and legal compliance.

If you uninstall the app, locally stored preferences and PDF files are removed with it. To request deletion of analytics or crash data, contact us at the address below.

6. Children's privacy

The app is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has used the app and you have concerns, contact us and we will take appropriate action.

7. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. When we do, the effective date at the top will change. If you have accepted a previous version, the app will prompt you to review and accept the updated policy on your next launch.
